Our microcontroller.We chose an Arduino Uno.

We powered it with an ATX supply.

We were provided with an Arduino Uno at the start of the project and saw no need to change. The Arduino, equipped with a Protoshield designed by Olin's own Prof. Brad Minch, connects to each of the 4 servos, as well as our solenoid and potentiometer.

Our solenoid system. It goes click.

The solenoid is screwed into the sprinker valves.

After purchasing our sprinkler valve, we discovered the solenoid runs on 24VAC. Rather than finding a new solenoid to interface with the valve, we designed a simple circuit to run the solenoid. The circuit uses a commonly avaliable 2N7000 MOSFET to drive a 5V relay which switches a 24VAC transformer that the team found (we think it originally powered stereo equipment). The MOSFET and Arduino are protected with a diode accross the relay. (Click on the schematic for a larger version)

Our pan and tilt system. It pans and tilts.

We could have called it azimuth and elevation, but then it seems even more like an artillery piece.

Our two-axis actuation is, again, powered with servos. The panning uses a continuous rotation standard-sized servo coupled to an angle-feedback potentiometer. To tilt the barrel, we used a high-torque metal-gear analog servo geared down for 60 degrees of range. The servo is coupled to the barrel through two billet aluminium blocks, one of which is supported with a bearing.
